FAQ

Is Xylume a blockchain?

Xylume is not a blockchain. However, it’s a functioning decentralized ledger based on a DAG with full support for validation, P2P networking, transaction flow, tokens, contracts, mining and everything — just not on mainnet yet. Tokens have no monetary value, but everything else works for real.

Can I use Ethereum tools and wallets like ethers.js or MetaMask?

Yep. Xylume TestNet is EVM-compatible, so you can connect with MetaMask, WalletConnect, or any EVM-compatible wallet. Ethereum libraries and tools such as ethers and web3 are also compatible with Xylume.

Are the test tokens worth anything?

Nope. TestNet tokens (XYL) are for testing, playing around, development and experimentation only. They hold no real value and cannot be traded for money.

Can I deploy smart contracts?

Absolutely. Xylume supports most of the EVM stack. You can deploy and interact with contracts just like you would on Ethereum or any EVM-based chain.

Smart contracts are temporarily disabled

The contract logic is currently under revision and testing. It’ll be re-enabled soon with improvements and stability fixes. Native core contracts, reserve addresses, and tokens deployed via the Native Token Handler remain unaffected.

How do I get test tokens?

Use our faucet — just enter your wallet address and you’ll get some XYL to play with. Faucet link is in the Getting Started section.

Can I run a node or mine?

Yes. You can run your own node and even mine transactions using the official miner. Links to guides and tools are available throughout the docs.

Will this become a mainnet?

That's the goal. Xylume TestNet is a full simulation of how the mainnet would work. Once liquidity and deployment resources are ready, the mainnet will follow — with possible upgrades and a fresh genesis.
