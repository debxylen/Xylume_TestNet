Tokenomics & Gas

Xylume TestNet uses a dynamic gas model designed to reward active nodes and miners fairly, while also preventing spam and overuse of network resources. Every transaction consumes gas, paid in the native Xylume (XYL) token.

Dynamic Gas Fees

Xylume's base gas fee increases with network load — adapting to demand to maintain performance and discourage spam.

Gas Calculation

The base gas cost is dynamically adjusted based on the current incoming transaction rate:

formula 
BaseFee = 0.000069 + (0.000069 * (IncomingTPS / 10000))

Incoming TPS

Incoming transactions per second (TPS) is derived from the number of pending transactions in the mempool and their timestamps:

formula 
IncomingTPS = PendingTxCount / (LatestTxTimestamp - OldestTxTimestamp)

Token Supply

The initial supply of the native XYL token is 6,942,340 XYL. It uses 18 decimal places, with the smallest unit called wxei, equivalent to 1e-18 XYL, similar to Ether’s smallest unit 'wei'.

Note

Tokenomics on the TestNet are experimental and non-monetary. Token supply, fees, and reward rates may change prior to mainnet.

The dynamic gas mechanism also deters spam attacks by making frequent or large transaction batches more costly — while keeping regular usage cheap. It's a balance between flexibility, fairness, and performance.
